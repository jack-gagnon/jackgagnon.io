Waterloo, Belgium

The soil that touches my feet is coated in thick green grass. The air is filled meticulously with particles, that smell like surrounding flowers, flora and fauna. I walk down the street and look upwards. I see clouds… the glance is held just long enough – one superior drop of rain falls and hits me between the eyes. If we’re lucky, it’ll stop next week. I need Vitamin C.



Bird’s Eye View

4000 ft. above, street lights twinkle like little stars. Roads and major highways appear as a simple map. Clouds make up a soft white mattress, blanketing the horizon, as we climb higher. Closer to heaven than ever. A gentle breeze and millions of dollars’ worth of jet equipment keep us aloft. Fuck permanent wings. I’ve got my mind.



Amalfi Coast

Freshly paved roads wind around steep mountain slopes. Houses, buildings, and churches nestled into the extremely high-altitude cliffs. Locals drive at 30km, tourists cruise at 5. Mopeds are seen whizzing through the traffic. From the tops of the terraces shines the magnificent blue coastal water. It shines like an animation of fairy dust across gentle waves. As I look up, I see the mountains dotted with caves and caverns. The pavement; a long-boarder’s paradise. Winding turns, fresh asphalt. And as the sun sets, the full moon lights the way.
