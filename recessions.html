This is an extremely simplistic model of the relationship between Google search interest in the term "recession" and the adjusted close of the Dow Index.

 

The chart depicts the (annual) average adjusted close of the Dow Jones Industrial index against the average search interest in the keyword "recession" across time, exlusive to the United States. We can see keyword search frequency spike in 2007, and into 2009-10. The trend mirrors a decline in the DJI's adjusted close. It should be noted - the scale of the two integer variables does not reflect the true impact of the period's recession.

 

Despite the simplicity, the chart highlights how our conceptualization of the state of financial markets can exacerbate what happens within.

 

The visual was created with the intention of highlighting the possible social tipping point of recession within financial markets, and by extension, our economy.
